Selection and Validation of TUSCO Genes

Our rigorous multi-step process ensures that TUSCO genes provide a reliable standard for evaluating transcript identification in long-read RNA sequencing.

1
Candidate Gene Selection
Genes must have a single annotated isoform with identical splice sites, transcription start sites (TSS), and transcription termination sites (TTS) across multiple annotation databases.
2
Annotation Consistency Validation
Validation using GENCODE12 and RefSeq13 databases.
3
Alternative Splicing Check
Filtering process to exclude genes with potential unannotated splice sites.
4
Expression Analysis
Assessment of broad expression across tissues using data from GTEx, ENCODE, and HRT Atlas.

Selection Criteria

Single Isoform

Genes must have only one annotated transcript to ensure unambiguous benchmarking.

Identical Splice Sites

All exon-intron boundaries must be consistent across annotation databases.

Consistent TSS & TTS

Transcription start and termination sites must be identical across databases.
